Tabi Boots

Over 500 years old and still going strong

Here's a timeline of the Tabi:

  • 15th century - predecessor of the tabi was introduced to the island of Japan from China via the Korean Peninsula
  • 1592 - the tabi design went from a sock to the split toe design to accommodate the popular thong style sandals of the time
  • Early 1600s - the tabi were used not just as socks, so they started making them out of leather and other materials and called this style jikatabi, which means "tabi that touch the ground"
  • 1657 - a giant fire caused the price of leather to increase leading to a change in construction materials to cotton and various sole materials like straw and canvas
  • 1922 - Tokujiro and Shojiro Ishibashi, founders of Bridgestone tires, were credited as inventing the very first tabi boots with rubber outsoles and was a really popular choice for manual labor jobs like construction, rickshaw drive, and agricultural work
  • 1942 - the tabi boots wer used by a lot of the military for off-duty wear and where the West gets exposed to the tabi boot in a battle in New Guinea for the first time
  • 1949 - the shoe company Onitsuka Tiger is founded and dabbles in making a split toe tabi running shoe
  • 1951 - a Japanese marathon racer, Shigeki Tanaka, wins the Boston Marathon wearing a pair of split the toe shoes
  • 1980s - fashion designer Martin Margiela visits Japan and has the idea to take this split-toe tabi boot and make it a high heel and mini
  • 1989 - despite pushback from cobblers, the designer's inaugural show debuted the split toe boot and became a polarizing classic immediately
  • 1996 - Nike tries their hand at a minimalist running tabi style shoe with the Air Rift which became a cult hit
  • 2006 - Onitsuka Tiger, in collaboration with JAXA, tries to make a shoe for astronauts to wear on the journey to the ISS
  • 2023 - you can buy a pretty wide variety of tabi boots and styles inlcude: steel toe tabi, minimalist tabi, tabi with laces up the front, and slip-on tabi
